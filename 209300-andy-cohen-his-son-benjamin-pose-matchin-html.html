COHEN MATCHES IN PJ'S WITH BEN

"Three cheers for PJ Party!" Cohen captioned the shot. In the pictures, the father-son duo wore matching pajama sets splattered with green leaves.

The heartwarming duo appeared to be clapping their hands in both shots. Ben, 15 months, wore a toothy smile as he looked up at his dad in the first picture.

In the second, Cohen looked down at him. The post received nearly 364k on the first day, with many of Cohen's celebrity colleagues commenting underneath.

COHEN HAPPILY REUNITED WITH BEN

The "Watch What Happens Live with Andy Cohen" host recently reunited with his son after a nearly two-week stint in self-isolation when he tested positive for COVID-19.

The separation was in March, and Cohen returned to living with his son at the end of the month. "I've hosted reunions for years, but yesterday's was the best one yet," he said at the time.

Soon enough, Ben might have a playmate around his own age. When things go back to normal, his father is hoping that Ben and Anderson Cooper's new son will be two peas in a pod.

BEN AND WYATT TO BE BFF'S

The reporter welcomed his first child earlier this month. Responding to a comment made on Instagram, Cohen approved the suggestion that Ben and Wyatt would be best friends.

Andy Cohen giving a thumbs up on his Instagram Story | Photo: Instagram/@andycohen

Before that, Cohen had commented underneath Cooper's post announcing the new members of his family. "I know a friend who can't wait to meet you!" he reportedly wrote.

Ben and Wyatt already have something in common, apart from their famous dads. During his Sirius XM radio show, Cohen revealed that his son's former nanny is now the nanny for Cooper's child.

Fans of "Radio Andy" guessed as much when Cohen kept saying that the woman was leaving him to go and help a friend of his. Cohen noted that they took precautions during the process because of COVID-19.

COHEN DENIED ABILITY TO DONATE

While most things are going right for the entertainer, there's also some bad news to share. Back in April, Cohen revealed how he was deemed ineligible to give his blood in order to assist in COVID-19 research.

Cohen alleged that the reason the FDA rejected was that he is a gay man, and they wanted to avoid the potential risk of spreading HIV. Furthermore, gay men have to abstain from sex for three months before donations.

Cohen noted that no such rules existed for straight individuals, and questioned the logic applied in the protocols. He lamented the lack of adaptation as he explained that all he wanted was to save a life.
