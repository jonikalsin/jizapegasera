Kaya Scodelario

Kaya Rose Scodelario is an English actress. She is best known for her roles in “Skins,” “The Maze Runner,” and “Pirates of the Caribbean” as Effy Stonem, Teresa Agnes, and Carina Barbossa. She appeared in Robbie Williams’ “Candy” music video.

Where was Kaya Scodelario born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Kaya Scodelario was born in Haywards Heath, West Sussex, England, on March 13, 1992. She was born under the sign of Pisces and is 30 years old. She was born in Ihu, Sao Paulo, to a British father Roger Humphrey, and a Brazilian mother Katia Scodelario.

Her mother’s Italian grandfather gave her the surname. Kaya’s parents divorced when she was still a child. Kaya was raised in London by her mother. She eventually adopted her surname and became fluent in Portuguese.

How did Kaya Scodelario start her Professional Career?

Kaya Scodelario debuted as Effy Stonem in ‘Skins’ at the age of 14 despite having no acting experience. She became dissatisfied because she thought she was too young to be there, but the producer encouraged her to stay. Later, her character had few speaking lines, which increased significantly when she became the main character in 2008.

Scodelario stayed on the show until the fourth season when she was replaced by a new cast. She reprised her role in ‘Fire,’ one of three episodes in the seventh season of ‘Skins.’ She claims that ‘Fire’ is more like a film, and she can relate to Effy’s struggle to mature from a teenager to an adult.

In 2009, Scodelario made her film debut in the science fiction thriller ‘Moon.’ She also appeared in the 2010 film ‘Shank,’ as Tasha. In 2010, she portrayed Peshet in Clash of the Titans. Kaya confirmed her role as Cathy in ‘Wuthering Heights’ the following year. She has been described as a “heartbreaking revelation.” Kaya was cast as Sally Weaver in the British thriller ‘Twenty8k’ in 2011.

The following year, she agreed to star in ‘Now Is Good,’ a film based on Jenny Downham’s novel ‘Before I Die.’ In 2019, Scodelario portrayed Carole Ann Boone in Extremely Wicked, Shockingly Evil, and Vile. Later that year, she starred in the horror film ‘Crawl,’ was cast in the drama ‘Spinning Out,’ and filmed ‘The Pale Horse’ for Amazon and BBC.
